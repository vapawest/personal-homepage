Valerie West

About The Artist

Valerie West is a fourth-year BFA student at the University of Cincinnati's College of Design, Architecture, Art, and Planning. Originally rooted in video and sound art, her practice expanded beyond the digital when she joined a combat robotics team. Beginning to design 3D objects within manufacturing and material limitations.

Now working as a sculptor and fiber artist, Valerie merges traditional craft with technological process. Juxtaposing industrial fabrication methods with handmade making to produce objects that embody the process of becoming. Caught between the digital and the physical, her work carries evidence of both origins: the elegance of a CAD file, the logic of a G-Code Path, and the unmistakable trace of material still in the act of becoming.

Valerie started the Art + Technology Club (ATC) at DAAP along with two of her peers, Haley Zuch and Valeria Casas-Lopez. The goal of the club is to create an extra-curricular space for students in the electronic art path of fine arts, but does not exclude non-FA majors or even non-DAAP majors. Everyone is welcome. The club hosts lectures, software tutorials, and critique group.

Valerie comes from a background of sound and video art, originally looking to go to school for music. She has been playing instruments since she was 9 years old. She began taking art classes her senior year of high school and this is what put her on the path of pursuing fine arts for college.
